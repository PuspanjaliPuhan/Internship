A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931 - 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as India's 11th president from 2002 to 2007. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. Kalam is known as the "Missile Man" for his role in India's missile technology, designing the Agni and Prithvi ballistic missiles, and developing India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III. He also helped establish India as a nuclear power, oversaw the development of several indigenous missiles, and played a key role in increasing cooperation between India and Russia in the field of space research.
